# More advanced: Transitions

<ul>
    <li>Issue reopens vs Issue resolutions</li>

    <li>...while a persistent upward trend shows low quality work</li>

# Prioritization

<div style={{ margin: "0", display: "flex" }}>
  <div style={{ width: "50%", margin: "0 1%", textAlign: "center" }}>
    <p>Easy and Fast<br />but very rough and subjective</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: "20px", color: "#B6B6B6" }}>
      <b>HiPPO rule</b><br />"Highest Paid Person‚Äôs Opinion"
      <br /><br />
      <b>Length Variable</b>:<br />"The longest first,<br />then the medium-sized tasks,<br />and finally the smallest". 
      <br /><br />
      <b>MoSCoW rule</b><br />"Must have, should have, could have, would have"
    </p>

    <p>More accurate<br />but more difficult</p>

      <b>CD3 rule</b><br />Cost of Delay Divided Duration
      <br /><br />
      <b>Weighted Shortest Job First</b>:<br />Businees Value + Time + Risk Reduction<br />-------------------------------------------<br />Job Size (length)
      <br /><br />
      <b>Cost Benefit Analysis</b>
    </p>

## Code Review /2

<div style={{ margin: "0 5%", display: "flex" }}>
  <div style={{ width: "50%" }}>
    <p style={{ marginBottom: "10px" }}>Bads</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: "20px", color: "#B6B6B6", marginTop: "5px" }}>
      Commands and authority: "you must change it!"
      <br /><br />
      Arrogance or offensive: "What a f... is this?"
      <br /><br />
      Judge the person: "Are you stupid? This implementation means you aren't a good developer..."
      <br /><br />
      Distractive comments, too many jokes or mockery, GIF, ironic videos...<br />
      ok with some emoticons!
      <br /><br />
      At the beginning they seems nice...<br />but in the long time they could cause misunderstanding beetween the people
    </p>
  </div>
  <div style={{ width: "50%" }}>
    <p style={{ marginBottom: "10px" }}>Goods</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: "20px", color: "#B6B6B6", marginTop: "5px" }}>
      Principles, patterns, best practices
      <br /><br />
      Examples, links, materials
      <br /><br />
      Inclusion: "what do you think if...?"
      <br /><br />
      Ask for a third opinion: "you did this, I think that, we can ask to..."
      <br /><br />
      A branch over the current PR
      <br /><br />
      Clear and not vauge comments<br />‚Üí "the line 123 has this specific problem" and not "it is confusing" "I'd try a better solution"
      <br /><br />
      If something could be improved, ok, maybe approve now but open a tech debt task immediately<br /> ‚Üí often "after" means "never"
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
