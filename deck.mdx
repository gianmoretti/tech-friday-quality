# Metrics: Management process

<div style={{ margin: "0", display: "flex" }}>
  <div style={{ width: "30%", margin: "0 1%", textAlign: "center"}}>
    <p>Defect density</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: "20px", color: "#B6B6B6" }}>
      Number of bugs as absolute number along the time
      <br /><br />
      ...better if scaled to the Number of story points
      <br /><br />
       ➡️ Look at the trend: if it increase maybe we're going too fast!!!
      <br /><br />
      Note: it's like the Coronavirus: the actions of Today will have effect in a couple of week...
    </p>
  </div>
  <div style={{ width: "30%", margin: "0 1%", textAlign: "center" }}>
    <p>Team reliability</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: "20px", color: "#B6B6B6" }}>
      Committment / Actual completion ratio
      <br /><br />
      Good or bad estimations
      <br /><br />
      Team affinity
    </p>
  </div>
  <div style={{ width: "30%", margin: "0 1%", textAlign: "center" }}>
    <p>Team Internal Organization</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: "20px", color: "#B6B6B6" }}>
      Sprint burndown report
      <br /><br />
      The long, difficult tasks first, and then the medium-sized tasks, and finally the smaller ones. 
      <br /><br />
      MoSCoW rule: "Must have, should have, could have, would have" 
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

# More in concrete: Transitions

<ul>
    <li>Issue reopens vs Issue resolutions</li>
    <Steps>
    <li>A declining re-open ratio shows work quality is improving</li> 
    <li>...while a persistent upward trend shows low quality work</li>
    </Steps>
</ul>

*Tools*: JIRA reports (with specific plugins tracking the transition number)
